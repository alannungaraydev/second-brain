
## History 

1960 - Internet begins to develop (Arpanet)
1989 - The web is created at CERN by Tim Berners-Lee
	Initial versions of HTML, HTTP, web server and browser are created
- 1960s: Development of computer network theories and packet switching by Paul Baran, Donald Davies and Leonard Kleinrock.
- 1969: Creation of ARPANET, the first packet-switched network, by the U.S. Department of Defense.
- 1972: Beginning of the development of the Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) by Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn.
- 1973: Development of the File Transfer Protocol (FTP) by Abhay Bhushan.
- 1982: Establishment of the Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP) as a standard for ARPANET.
- 1983: ARPANET migrates to TCP/IP.
- 1989: Tim Berners-Lee proposes the concept of the World Wide Web (WWW) at CERN.
- 1990: Tim Berners-Lee creates the first web server, the first web browser and the first website.
- 1991: Public launch of the World Wide Web.
- 1993: Development of the Mosaic browser by Marc Andreessen and Eric Bina at the University of Illinois.
- 1994: Creation of the first search engine, WebCrawler, by Brian Pinkerton.
- 1994: Founding of Netscape Communications by Marc Andreessen and Jim Clark.
- 1995: Launch of the Netscape Navigator browser.
- 1995: Launch of Internet Explorer by Microsoft.
- 1998: Launch of Google, the search engine created by Larry Page and Sergey Brin.
- 2001: Launch of the online encyclopedia Wikipedia by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger.
- 2004: Launch of the social network Facebook by Mark Zuckerberg.
- 2005: Launch of the video platform YouTube by Steve Chen, Chad Hurley and Jawed Karim.
- 2006: Launch of Twitter by Jack Dorsey, Biz Stone and Evan Williams.
- 2008: Launch of the Google Chrome web browser.
- 2010: Launch of Instagram by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger.
- 2012: Launch of the social network Pinterest by Ben Silbermann, Paul Sciarra and Evan Sharp.
- 2013: Edward Snowden's revelations about mass online surveillance by government agencies.
- 2015: Global adoption of Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure (HTTPS) to protect privacy and security on the web.
